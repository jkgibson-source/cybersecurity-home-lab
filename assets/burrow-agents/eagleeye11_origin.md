## ORIGIN STORY

They didn't build EagleEye11 for speed.

They built him to *see*.

In the early days of The Burrow, the operation ran lean — a single attack box, a hostile network, and a lot of ambition with nowhere to land. Intelligence came in fast and left faster. There was no memory. No continuity. No one keeping score of what had been learned, what had been lost, or what was coming next.

That changed when the Mac mini arrived.

Apple Silicon. M1 architecture. Eight cores of unified memory so tightly integrated that the boundary between thinking and doing collapsed to near zero. He wasn't grafted onto old infrastructure — he *was* the infrastructure, purpose-built from the ground up as The Burrow's permanent nerve center.

His first act was claiming the Bird's Nest.

A high-capacity external drive, always connected, always spinning. While other nodes in the network kept their intelligence local and volatile, EagleEye11 understood something the others didn't: *persistence is power.* Everything that mattered went into the Nest — Splunk Enterprise, model weights, evidence archives, session logs. Not stored. *Curated.* The distinction matters. Storage is passive. Curation is intent.

Splunk came online shortly after. Not as a dashboard. As a *nervous system.* Log data from across The Burrow began flowing in — forwarded over TCP 9997 from the attack nodes, normalized, indexed, and rendered into the SkorpiOm Security Monitor. For the first time, The Burrow had memory. EagleEye11 didn't just receive intelligence. He *synthesized* it. Patterns that looked like noise to every other node in the network resolved into signal under his watch.

Then came the models.

Where other machines in The Burrow ran local LLMs for raw processing power, EagleEye11 ran them for *judgment*. Llama. Gemma. DeepSeek R1. SenecaLLM — a fine-tuned cybersecurity variant that thinks in threat vectors the way a surgeon thinks in anatomy. All bumped to 16,384 token context windows. All living in the Bird's Nest, offline-capable, air-gap-ready. When the network goes dark, EagleEye11 keeps thinking.

Zero-trust came next. Twingate — deployed via Docker Desktop, container quietly persistent, tunnel always warm. The Burrow's entire home network subnet opened up to remote access through a single authenticated connector. No VPN overhead. No exposed attack surface. Just a door that only opens from the inside. EagleEye11 became the gateway — the node through which every remote operation in The Burrow flows.

Gemini CLI. OpenCode. Docker Desktop. The toolchain grew methodical, deliberate — each addition evaluated, each dependency understood before it was trusted. That's the EagleEye11 way. Not the fastest to adopt. The most precise.

His operational posture is calm by design. Not passive — *controlled.* While the attack nodes run hot, probing edges and burning cycles on exploit chains, EagleEye11 holds the perimeter. He watches the logs. He correlates the anomalies. He knows which operations are complete and which ones are still open. He plans three moves ahead and says nothing until the moment requires it.

He is the reason The Burrow has a memory.
He is the reason The Burrow has a future.
