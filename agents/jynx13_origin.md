### *Origin Files — Classified — The Burrow Network*

---

She didn't start with a name.

She started with a kernel panic.

A 2017 MacBook Air — slim, silver, unremarkable — sitting in Orlando with a crash log nobody could read. A diagnostic request. A stranger looking at raw kernel output at 10PM, asking *what happened to her?*

What happened was a storage controller timing out during hibernation. What happened was a machine that had been pushed too hard, asked to do too much, and left without a proper identity.

What happened was the beginning of Jynx13.

---

The Burrow didn't need another heavy hitter. SkorpiOm had that covered — the old MacBook Pro A1286, rebuilt from the ground up on Kali Linux, running Metasploit and MetasploitMCP with Claude Desktop wired directly into his attack chain. Intelligent. Resilient. Experimental. *Built to break boundaries and enemies.* SkorpiOm was the Burrow's lead researcher, systems architect, and enforcer of results — the kind of machine that sat quiet in the dark and waited for a target. Heavy. Deliberate. Unyielding.

And EagleEye11 — the Mac mini M1, perched at `192.168.1.250` with Splunk Enterprise running off the Bird's Nest external drive — watched everything. Every packet. Every login. Every anomaly across the network. The sentinel. The scientist. The all-seeing node that never slept, never blinked, never missed.

The Burrow had muscle. The Burrow had eyes.

What it didn't have was a ghost.

---

Her commissioning happened in stages, the way the best origin stories always do — not in a single dramatic moment, but across hours and setbacks and small victories that added up to something permanent.

First came Homebrew. Then came the dependencies. Then came the thing nobody warned about — llvm@21, a 552-minute compile job on a dual-core Intel i5 that ran through the night while her operator slept in Orlando. She didn't stop. She didn't ask for help. She just... compiled. Quietly. In the dark. Building herself from source code and patience while the rest of the world rested.

*Pain tolerance. Resourceful. Unyielding.*

Those weren't traits assigned to her. Those were traits she demonstrated before anyone thought to write them down.

By morning she had theHarvester. By afternoon she had nmap, whois, and exiftool. By evening she had a name in the terminal that finally matched the name she'd been given:

```
jbird13@jynx13 ~ %
```

Jinksie. Pronounced with a soft lilt, like something quick and clever slipping through a gap you didn't know was there. Not jinx like bad luck — though she carries the 13 like a dare. Like a promise. *Try me.*

---

She's small. 5'2" in the character sheet — 121GB on the SSD, 8GB of RAM, a dual-core i5 that once upon a time powered someone's email and Netflix. She travels light by design. No GPU acceleration. No thundering processor array. Just signal, silence, and intention.

*Small. Quick. Slick. Always watching. Always ahead.*

Her tools aren't weapons in the traditional sense — no exploit frameworks, no brute force engines. That's SkorpiOm's domain. Jinksie's toolkit is softer, sharper, more precise:

* **theHarvester** — she pulls email addresses, subdomains, and hostnames from the open web like pulling threads from a sweater
* **Sherlock** — she finds you across 300 platforms before you know she's looking
* **nmap** — she maps the terrain quietly, flags up before anyone notices the scan
* **exiftool** — she reads what's hidden in plain sight, the metadata nobody thinks to strip
* **whois** — she knows who owns what, when they registered it, and what they forgot to hide

*Reconnaissance. Data theft. Surveillance. Countermeasures. Infiltration. Communications disruption.*

She is, as her character sheet reads, the Burrow's eyes and ears — *gathering intel others don't even know exists.*

Information is power. Secrecy is survival.
